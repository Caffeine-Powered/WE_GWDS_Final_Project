Axial Systems

Axial typographic systems are designs that branch from a central line. They can be symmetrical or asymmetrical around the central line, or axis they are based off of, allowing for the creation of neat and ordered designs whilst also being able to create “messy” or more unorthodox designs.

Radial Systems

Radial typographic systems are designs that originate and revolve around a central point. These designs are useful for drawing eyes to a certain point and creataing a dynamic visual experience.

Dialatational Systems

The dialatational typographic system expands from a centre point in a circular fashion. Different parts can originate from different points allowing for multiple circular designs.

Random Systems

A random typography system is elements organsied randomly. This can include elements intersecting each other or being inverted to the point of illegibility. Using this system creates an inherently chaotic design.

Grid Systems

The grid typographic system is elements organised into columbs and rows. This system can produce very organised work, and is primarily used in articles and textbooks due to its easy to navigate structure.

Modular Systems

Modular typographic systems is a system that groups elements together in seperate sections. This system whilst similar in nature to the grid typographic system, can be organised much more free form in where the modules are placed.

Transitional Systems

Transition typographic systems are much more free flowing than other systems, favouring the design of the piece rather than the legibility. Transitional pieces don’t follow a specific axis and therefore can appear to flow together.

Bilateral Systems

The bilateral typographic system rather simply means that the elements are centred and symmetrical on the page.
